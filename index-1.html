The 7-Step Go-To-Market Playbook
From zero to your first paying client in 2 weeks.
01
Foundation
Pick Your Niche Vertical
Don't sell to "small businesses." Pick one industry and go deep.
  • Restaurants / food & bev (highest pain, fastest close)
  • Local service businesses (plumbers, electricians, HVAC)
  • Real estate agencies
  • Medical / dental practices
  • Law firms (solo & small)
02
Validation
Find the $10K Problem
Interview 5 businesses in your niche before building anything. Ask:
  • "What task does your team hate doing most?"
  • "What's the last thing that fell through the cracks?"
  • "If you had an extra staff member, what would they do?"
  • Map time × wage × frequency to quantify the pain
03
Build
Build the MVP in One Day
Claude Artifacts = zero infrastructure. Start simple.
  • Pick one product from the catalog (Artifact first)
  • Customize branding, prompts, and data for their business
  • Add their logo, colors, and example data
  • Time target: 4–8 hours for first version
04
Proof
Offer One Free Pilot
Give one business a 2-week free trial in exchange for:
  • A video testimonial on results
  • Permission to use their name & metrics
  • A referral to one other business owner
  • Document everything as a case study
05
Package
Create Your "Menu"
Bundle products into tiers, not à la carte.
  • Starter: 1 Artifact + setup + 30-day support
  • Growth: 3 Artifacts + custom training + monthly check-in
  • Enterprise: Full MCP integration + dedicated build hours
  • Add retainer: "AI updates & optimization" $200–$500/mo
06
Sell
Lead With the ROI Story
Never lead with "AI" — lead with the outcome.
  • "Save 8 hours/week on customer replies" not "AI chatbot"
  • Show the demo in the first 60 seconds of every meeting
  • Anchor against hiring: "$1,500/mo vs $4,500/mo for staff"
  • Use the case study from Step 4 as social proof
07
Scale
Productize & Repeat
Once 3+ clients are on the same product, systematize it.
  • Build an onboarding template (Notion or PDF)
  • Create a customization checklist per industry
  • Hire a VA to handle setup for new clients
  • Add MCP integrations to justify price increases

Pricing Psychology Tips
  • Always quote annual value destroyed, not monthly cost
  • Offer a 14-day "results guarantee" — reduces risk objection
  • Bundle support retainer into setup fee: looks like better value
  • Use odd pricing ($497 not $500) to signal precise costing
  • Charge more to restaurants than solo service businesses
Revenue Model Benchmarks

  • Add upsell: "AI audit" report at $350 one-time
Sales Scripts & Cold Outreach
Proven frameworks for each stage of the sales process. Customize with the client's name and specific pain point.
Cold DM / Email — Subject: Quick question about [Business Name]
Hi [Name], I noticed [Business Name] has been around for [X years] — impressive. I help [restaurants / service businesses / etc.] in [city] save 6–10 hours per week by automating the tasks your team dreads. I built a quick demo specifically for a business like yours. Would you be open to a 15-minute screen share this week? I'll show you the demo even if you decide it's not for you. No pitch deck, just the tool running live. — [Your name]
Discovery Call — Opening Frame
"Before I show you anything, I want to make sure this is actually relevant for you. Can I ask — what's the most repetitive thing your team does every week that feels like it should be handled automatically by now? [Let them talk for 2+ minutes. Take notes. Reflect back the exact words they use.] Got it. That's exactly the kind of thing I've been building solutions for. Let me show you what I built for a [similar business] last month — took them from 3 hours a day on that to about 20 minutes."
Objection: "We already use [ChatGPT / some tool]"
"That's great — most of my best clients started there. The difference is that what I build is trained specifically on your business: your menu, your prices, your tone, your common customer questions. Generic AI gives generic answers. What I build feels like your best employee — the one who never leaves and never forgets anything. Can I show you the difference in 5 minutes? I'll put your actual business info in and you can see it live."
Objection: "It's too expensive"
"I hear you. Let me put it in perspective. You mentioned [task] takes about [X hours/week] across your team. At [$Y/hr], that's [$Z/month] in staff time — and that doesn't include the mistakes or the things that fall through the cracks. The Growth plan is $497/month. That's [fraction] of what you're already spending. And if it doesn't save you at least that in time in the first 30 days, I'll refund the setup fee, no questions. Does that feel fairer?"
